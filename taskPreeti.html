About Chhattisgarh

Chhattisgarh, located in the heart of India, is the 10th largest state by area, covering 135,194 km2 (52,199 sq mi). Home to 32.2 million people, it ranks as the 17th most populous state. Bordered by Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Telangana, Odisha, Jharkhand, and Uttar Pradesh, this landlocked state is a treasure trove of culture, nature, and history, with 28 districts offering diverse experiences.

Bhoramdeo Temple

Bhoramdeo Temple, known as the 'Khajuraho of Chhattisgarh', is a stunning ancient Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. Built between the 7th and 11th centuries A.D., this temple is located amidst the mountain ranges of Kabirdham. Its resemblance to the Sun Temple of Konark and the Khajuraho Temples adds to its artistic appeal.

Mainpat

Mainpat, a lush green plateau surrounded by jungles, is a romantic destination perfect for honeymoons. Known as the 'Mini Tibet' due to the Tibetan settlements, and the 'Shimla of Sarguja' because of its natural beauty, Mainpat remains an unexplored hill station with mesmerizing hill slopes, waterfalls, and jungles.

Pamed Wildlife Sanctuary

Pamed Wildlife Sanctuary, located in the Dantewada district, covers 262 sq km and is home to a rich array of flora and fauna. Visitors can experience the dense mixed forests and spot deer, including the chital and chinkara, in this enchanting wildlife reserve that offers a true wilderness experience.
